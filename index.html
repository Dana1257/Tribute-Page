A Tribute page on Luke Perry

Timeline

  • 1966: Born on October 11 in Mansfield, Ohio. (Birth name: Coy Luther Perry III)

  • 1984: Moved to Los Angeles with the intention of becoming an actor.

  • 1987: Got his first role in the soap opera "Loving". He played the character Ned Bates until 1988.

  • 1988: Played Kenny; a recurring role on "Another World". He played Kenny until 1989.

  • 1990: Got the most significant role of his career; Dylan McKay in the teen drama Beverly Hills 90210. During that year he was also in the film "Terminal Bliss".

  • 1992: Was in the film "Buffy the Vampire Slayer".

  • 1993: Got a voice role in "The Simpsons" episode titled: "Krusty Gets Kancelled". He played a parody version of himself.

  • Late 1990's to 2010's: Appeared in various tv shows and movies.

  • 2015: Became a spokesperson for campaigns requiring early testing for cancer. What prompted this was a colonoscopy test that revealed pre-cancerous growths in his body, that could have developed into colorectal cancer.

  • 2017: Was in the television series "Riverdale", which was an adaptation of "Archie Comics' Characters".

  • 2019: On February 27 he experienced a massive stroke and a few days later he had a second stroke. He passed away on March 4, 2019 at the age of 52 from complications following those 2 strokes.

Other Facts About Luke Perry

  • Was raised by his mother and stepfather and he wasn't close to his biological father.

  • Was a teen idol in the 1990's and the early 2000's.

  • Auditioned for several years without getting hired while working odd jobs. Those odd jobs included being an extra in music videos.

  • Was buried near his home in Vanleer, Tennessee. In the farm he had bought in 1995.

  • Had 2 children: Sophie Perry and Jack Perry.

  • Was engaged when he passed away.
